System Design Strategy UX/UI

Enterprise Design System

Role Product Owner / UX Lead
Timeline 2021 — 2025
Team Product UX

Impact

What We Achieved

- 80% Design Incosistencies
- 50% Design to Dev Handoff Time
- 40% Reduction in rework

Creating a centrallized design system has not only improved UX/UI experience consistency.

The system has had a transformational impact on the organization fostering collaboration between designers, developers and other functions.

It has created a financial efficiency due to the generated savings, icreased conversion rates and the ability of the team to do more faster.


Problem

Challenges and Pain Points

When creating e-commerce designs, multiple teams relied on a basic style guide, largely static and inconsistently applied across web properties.

As the product portfolio expanded, UI/UX inconsistencies, duplicated work and scalability issues emerged.

  • A fragmented digital ecosystem
  • 20 regional teams, 50 countries, 5 e-commerce platforms and CMSs
  • No internal ownership over design and technology
  • Ineffective design and development processes
  • Constant design rework

Approach

Unified Control Center

  • Headless infrastructure
  • Single CMS (Sanity) to power the web, app and instore experiences
  • Figma to build the UI library
  • Storybook for react components
  • Unified documentation site

Goals

Consistency and Efficiency

  • Establish consistency across brand design and digital experience
  • Create a coherent experience for the system users: designers, developers and content authors
  • Facilitate collaboration and contribution between multiple teams
  • Leverage the expertise of the extended team (Brand, Marketing)

Process

Integrated Design and Development Workflow

Lightweight, agile process to design, develop and document components coupled with research to understand the needs and friction points of the system users and continuous alignment with the business and engineering teams.

  1. Explore:UI audits, Research & Specs
  2. Prototype: Design, Prototype & User-testing
  3. Implement: Publish Components
  4. Test: Unit & Accessibility Testing
  5. Document: Figma, Storybook, Docs Site

Results

Metrics

The following metrics were used to benchmark the system succcess after the launch.

System Coverage

The percentage of live product screens built using design system components vs. custom, one-off code.

Time-to-Market

The average time it takes a sprint team to build a new feature before vs. after the design system is implemented.

Reduction in Bugs

The drop in visual, layout, or style-related bug tickets entering the QA pipeline.
