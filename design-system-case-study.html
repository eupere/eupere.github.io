Creating an Enterprise Design System

How we build scalable design system to drive organizational change.

Role Product Owner / UX Lead
Timeline 2021 — 2025
Team Global

Impact

Creating a centrallized design system has not only improved the customer experience consisteny, it has a transformational impact on the organization itself improving collaboration between designers and developers.

Problem

Challenges and Pain Points

When creating e-commerce designs, multiple teams relied on a basic style guide, largely static and inconsistently applied across web properties. As the product portfolio expanded, UI/UX inconsistencies, duplicated work and scalability issues emerged.

  • A fragmented digital ecosystem: 20 regional teams, 50 countries, 5 e-commerce platforms and content management systems
  • No internal ownership over design and technology
  • Ineffective design and development processes
  • Constant design rework

Roles

Responsibilities and Teams

  • Core UX/UI team
  • External development parnter
  • Regional e-commerce teams

Approach

Unified Control Center

  • Headless infrastructure
  • Single CMS (Sanity) to power the web, app and instore experiences and documentation sites
  • Figma to build the UI library
  • Storybook for react components

Goals

Consistency and Efficiency

  • Establish consistency across brand design and digital experience
  • Create a coherent experience for the system users: designers, developers and content authors
  • Facilitate collaboration and contribution between multiple teams
  • Leverage the expertise of the extended team (Marketing, UX, SEO)

Process

Integrated Design and Development Workflow

Lightweight, agile process to design, develop and document components

  1. Explore:UI audits, research & specs
  2. Prototype: design, prototype & user-test
  3. Implement: publish components
  4. Test: unit & accessibility testing
  5. Document: in Figma, documentation site

System User Research

Understanding the needs and friction points of system users.

Workshops

Alignment with business and technology teams.

Results

Metrics

The following metrics were used to benchmark the system succcess after the launch.

System Coverage

The percentage of live product screens built using design system components vs. custom, one-off code.

Time-to-Market

The average time it takes a sprint team to build a new feature before vs. after the design system is implemented.

Reduction in Bugs

The drop in visual, layout, or style-related bug tickets entering the QA pipeline.
